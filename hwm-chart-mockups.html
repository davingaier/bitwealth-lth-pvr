High-Water Mark — Chart Mockups (Options A–E)

Sample data modelled on customer #49 (Tremyne Naidoo): two deposits (May & Jun), NAV currently just below the fee threshold. Fee threshold = HWM + contributions. HWM here is the profit-only baseline (= $0 until the client banks profit above contributions).

Option A — Fix the existing main-chart line minimum viable

Same combined chart as today, but the gold dashed series is corrected from bare high_water_mark_usd to the true Fee Threshold = HWM + contributions and relabelled. It reappears just above Cost Basis. Keep this regardless of what else we add.
Pros
  • One-line change, corrects the bug visually
  • No new UI to maintain
  • Customers keep the familiar combined view
Cons
  • Cluttered (5 series); threshold hugs Cost Basis
  • Doesn't emphasise "distance to HWM"

Option B — Dedicated "NAV vs High-Water Mark" chart recommended

Benchmarks removed for focus. Gold step line = Fee Threshold; NAV line is colour-segmented (green where in fee territory, amber where below); the gap is shaded so "headroom" is obvious at a glance.
Current NAV$41,959
Fee Threshold (HWM)$42,072
Distance to HWM−$113 (−0.3%)
Pros
  • Directly answers "how far am I from the HWM?"
  • Uncluttered, purpose-built
  • Shaded gap reads instantly
Cons
  • New chart to maintain
  • Threshold is steppy (ratchets monthly)

Option C — Profit-normalised view most rigorous

Subtract contributions from everything and plot profit only. Profit line = NAV − Cost Basis; HWM is already a profit concept so it lines up natively; zero = breakeven on contributions. The gap between profit and HWM is exactly "new profit before fees kick in."
View:
Pros
  • HWM finally looks meaningful (no contributions baked in)
  • Small dollar axis — easy to read
  • Mathematically honest representation of the fee rule
Cons
  • "Profit vs HWM" needs a one-line tooltip for less technical clients
  • Loses absolute portfolio size

Option D — Headroom oscillator simplest signal

A single derived series NAV − Fee Threshold as an area chart around a zero baseline. Positive (green) = fees accruing; negative (grey) = recovery needed before any fee.
Pros
  • Dead simple — one glance tells the story
  • Great as a compact strip / sparkline
Cons
  • Loses absolute context (portfolio size)
  • Less informative on its own

Option E — At-a-glance gauge (header KPI) best at-a-glance

Non-time-series. A horizontal bar positions today's NAV between Cost Basis (floor) and Fee Threshold (HWM). Pairs well as a header KPI above chart B or C.

Note: all five are driven by data already in get_customer_performance_data (nav_usd, cost_basis_usd, high_water_mark_usd).
