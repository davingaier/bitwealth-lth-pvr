3. OBJECTIVES OF THIS GUIDELINE
The objective of this user guide is to define and outline the steps to be followed when an
accountable institution or any other person that has made a report to the Financial
Intelligence Centre are required to submit additional information to the FIC in terms of
section 32 of the Financial Intelligence Centre Act No. 38 of 2001 (FIC Act). Section 32
of the FIC Act 38 of 2001 authorises the FIC to request additional information from
accountable institutions or any other person that has made a report in terms of section
28, 29 or 31 of the FIC Act to furnish the FIC with additional information concerning the
report. The additional information file transaction (AIFT) will be used to capture
transactional information as defined in the FIC Act on the reporting platform.
4. INTRODUCTION
The FIC has implemented an integrated software solution as its preferred platform for
registration, reporting, data collection, analysis, case management and secure
communications required for the FIC’s daily operational functions and requirements. The
registration and reporting platform is called goAML. Section 32 FIC Act 38 of 2001
authorises the FIC or an investigating authority acting with the permission of the FIC or
under the authority of an authorised officer, to request additional information from
accountable institutions or any other person that has made a report in terms of section
28, 29 or 31 of the FIC Act to furnish the FIC with additional information concerning the
report. The additional information file transaction (AIFT) will be used to capture
transactional information as defined in the FIC Act.

8.4.1 From Type / To Type
goAML V5.4 additional information file transaction user guide, version 1.2 3 September 2025
Page 14 of 27

Transactional reports on goAML are structured as bi-party transactions with a ‘From’
and ‘To’ part to every transaction. A transaction report will always have a ‘From’
(Payor/Source) and ‘To’ (Payee/Receiver) side which will need to be completed by the
reporter.
One side of the transaction, either the From Type or the To Type, have to be “my
client” or else the report will be rejected by the system. A reported subject, whether
"my client" or "not my client" will always be, either an account, entity and/or person. The
mandatory information fields that need to be completed will differ as per the FIC
requirements.
Party type
The Party Type refers to the source and destination party in relation to the AIFT being
reported to the FIC. This means the party to the ‘party type’ can be either a person,
account or entity. This means the source can be either a person, account or entity. This
also means that the destination can be either a person, account or entity.

10. SUBMITTING AN ADDITIONAL INFORMATION FILE TRANSACTION REPORT
TO THE FIC
Once all the required details have been provided, the bar on the left of the tabs will turn
green, to enable the user to submit the form.
Figure 23: Additional information file transaction report
Kindly note that once submitted, the report will be locked from being edited by the user.
Figure 24: AIFT submission confirmation screen
Once the form was submitted, the pop-up screen will display with the report reference
number. The reference number is used for any report related enquiries. A notification e-
mail with the refence number will be sent to the user’s e-mail address which was entered
during the registration process. The report receipt will be accessible via the goAML
message board. This receipt will indicate whether the submission was accepted or
rejected, helping ensure transparency and tracking.
