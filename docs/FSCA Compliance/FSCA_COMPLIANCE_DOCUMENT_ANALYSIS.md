# FSCA Compliance Document Analysis — BitWealth CASP FSP Licence
**Prepared:** March 2026  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive extraction and analysis of all 25 documents in the FSCA Compliance folder, for the purpose of applying for a CASP FSP licence under the FAIS Act (Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act, 2002, Act No. 37 of 2002).

---

## Executive Summary for BitWealth

BitWealth is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) that:
- Collects ZAR from clients
- Makes automated discretionary DCA decisions (buy/sell BTC) using the LTH PVR strategy
- Executes trades via VALR exchange on behalf of clients
- Manages client crypto assets in VALR subaccounts (omnibus / corporate account model)

**Applicable licence category:** Primarily **Category I** (advice + intermediary services in respect of Crypto Assets — sub-product 1.27). Potentially also **Category II** (discretionary management of crypto assets) if the automated DCA without per-trade client approval qualifies as discretionary management.

**Application fee (as at October 2024):** ZAR 2,697 for Category I FSP.

### Section 5: Financial Products Per Category

**For BitWealth: Applicable product = 1.27 Crypto Assets (Category I)**

- Type of service: Advice and/or Intermediary Services (non-automated or automated)
- Sub-product 1.27: **Crypto Assets**

### BitWealth Category Determination
| Scenario | Category |
|----------|---------|

**Recommendation:** Initially apply as **Category I** for crypto assets (1.27), with both advice and intermediary services. If the FSCA determines the automated DCA nature qualifies as discretionary, upgrade to also include Category II.

6. Breached a fiduciary duty
7. Been suspended, dismissed, or disqualified as director/managing executive/auditor
8. Been refused a licence/registration, or had one suspended/revoked
9. Been denied professional body membership or had it revoked
10. Been disciplined by a professional body or regulator
11. Knowingly provided false information to a regulator
12. Demonstrated unwillingness to comply with legal/regulatory requirements
13. Been found not fit and proper in any previous assessment
14. Been connected to a business that failed any question 1–13, or gone into liquidation/business rescue
15. Failed to disclose required information under the Act
16. Been prohibited from operating in the financial services industry
17. Been connected to an entity censured by a stock exchange/regulatory body
18. Aided/abetted breaches of laws/regulations
19. Been subject to investigation by any regulatory authority
20. Had its estate sequestrated/liquidated
21. Subject to pending proceedings that may lead to conviction/liability

## Document 11: FSP6.pdf — Compliance Officer (Phase 2 Approval)
**Type:** Application form  
**Pages:** 2

### Prerequisite
A compliance officer must have **Phase 1 approval** (via FSP 12) before the FSP can seek Phase 2 approval for that person.

### Section 1: Is a Compliance Officer Required?
Under **Section 17 of the FAIS Act**: Any FSP with more than one key individual OR one or more representatives MUST appoint a compliance officer.

**For BitWealth:** Almost certainly YES — will have at least one representative and/or multiple key individuals.

### Section 2: Internal vs External Compliance Officer
- **Internal**: Natural person in permanent employ of the FSP (or its subsidiary/holding company)
- **External**: Any other compliance officer, including a compliance practice

If Internal (2.1.1): Name, Phase 1 approval number, ID/Passport number  
If External, Natural Person (2.2.1): Name, Phase 1 approval number, ID/Passport number  
If External, Compliance Practice (2.2.2): Practice name, Phase 1 approval no., registration no., name of natural person appointed by the practice, their Phase 1 approval no., ID/Passport no.

### Section 3: Rendering Under Supervision?
If the compliance officer will be rendering services under supervision:
- Confirm supervisor's details (Phase 1 approval no., ID)
- Confirm whether supervisor is co-appointed as compliance officer
- Attach copy of supervision agreement

### Section 4: Annexure Required — Independence, Competency, Operational Ability
The following must be provided in a signed annexure:
- (a) Confirmation of adequate resources + matrix showing:
  - Number of FSPs categorised by type
  - Size of FSP (business premises, units, branches)
  - Number of representatives allocated to the compliance officer
  - Number and frequency of monitoring visits
  - Number and frequency of reports
- (b) Confirmation of direct access to and support from FSP's senior management
- (c) How independence and objectivity will be maintained
- (d) How conflicts of interest will be avoided
- (e) Monitoring plan (frequency of visits, training, services)
- (f) If internal: details of compliance function and organisational structure

### Section 5: Declaration by Compliance Officer
The compliance officer signs to confirm:
- Has required operational ability
- Can conduct regular reviews
- Can maintain records
- Can assist with compliance risk management strategy
- Can function independently and objectively
- Will avoid conflicts of interest

### BitWealth Options for Compliance Officer
1. **Appoint an external compliance practice** (recommended for a start-up without in-house compliance expertise). Must already have Phase 1 approval.
2. **Internal compliance officer** — a director who holds Phase 1 approval
3. **Director renders compliance under supervision** with an approved supervisor

## Document 19: a37-020.pdf — Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act, 2002 (Act No. 37 of 2002)
**Type:** Primary legislation — the foundational FAIS Act  
**Pages:** 37

### Part 1: Key Definitions (Section 1)

**"advice"** — any recommendation, guidance or proposal of a financial nature furnished to a client in respect of:
- (a) purchase of a financial product
- (b) investment in a financial product
- (c) conclusion of any other transaction (loan, cession) aimed at acquiring rights/benefits in a financial product
- (d) variation, replacement, or termination of a financial product

Regardless of whether it is given in the course of financial planning, or whether it actually results in a transaction.

**Advice EXCLUDES:**
- Factual advice on procedure, description, routine admin queries, objective information, display of promotional material
- Analysis/report without an implied recommendation
- Board advice to members of pension funds, friendly societies, medical schemes on their own benefits
- Other advisory activity exempted by the registrar

**"financial product"** includes:
- Securities and instruments (shares, debentures, money market, warrants, bonds)
- Short-term and long-term insurance
- Pension fund benefits
- Foreign currency denominated investments / deposits
- Health service benefits
- **Any other product declared by the registrar** (this is the mechanism by which crypto assets were added)
- Combined products / foreign products

**"financial services provider"** — any person who, as a regular feature of their business:
- (a) furnishes advice, OR
- (b) furnishes advice AND renders any intermediary service, OR
- (c) renders an intermediary service

**"intermediary service"** — any act (other than advice) performed for or on behalf of a client or product supplier, the result of which is that a client may enter into, or has entered into, a transaction in a financial product, OR with a view to:
- (b)(i) buying, selling, dealing, managing, administering, safekeeping, maintaining or servicing a financial product
- (b)(ii) collecting or accounting for premiums/moneys payable by client to product supplier
- (b)(iii) receiving, submitting or processing client claims

**Intermediary service EXCLUDES:**
- Bank acting as conduit between client and another product supplier
- Intermediary service rendered by a product supplier who is separately licensed under another law
- Other services exempted by the registrar

**"key individual"** — in relation to an FSP (or representative) that is a corporate/unincorporated body, trust, or partnership: any natural person responsible for managing or overseeing, either alone or with others, the activities relating to the rendering of any financial service.

**"representative"** — any person who renders a financial service to clients FOR OR ON BEHALF of an FSP, under conditions of employment or mandatory agreement. Excludes clerical/technical/administrative persons with purely subsidiary roles.

### Part 2: Chapter II — Authorisation (Sections 7–12)

**Section 7: Prohibition**
No person may act or offer to act as an FSP without a licence (from a date determined by the Minister).

**Section 8: Application for Authorisation**
Applications must satisfy the registrar that the applicant complies with:
- **(a) Personal character qualities** of honesty and integrity
- **(b) Competence and operational ability** to fulfil responsibilities under the Act
- **(c) Financial soundness**

For corporate/trust/partnership applicants: Additionally, each key individual must demonstrate (a) honesty and integrity AND (b) competence and operational ability (to the extent needed to fulfil their responsibilities).

The registrar may:
- Require additional information or verification
- Take into account other information from any source (if disclosed to applicant)
- Grant the application (with or without conditions) OR refuse

Conditions on a licence may include requirements relating to:
- Changes to key individuals (new KIs must be approved before taking part in management)

**Section 9: Suspension**
The registrar may suspend a licence if the FSP no longer meets the requirements of Section 8, after giving the FSP an opportunity to make submissions.

**Section 10: Withdrawal**
The registrar may withdraw a licence if:
- The applicant did not make full disclosure when applying, or provided false/misleading information
- The FSP has materially contravened the Act

**Section 11: Lapsing**
A licence lapses when:
- Natural person: becomes permanently incapacitated, sequestrated, or dies
- Company/trust: finally liquidated or dissolved
- Business becomes dormant
- Voluntary surrender

### Part 3: Chapter III — Representatives (Sections 13–14)

**Section 13: Qualifications of Representatives**
No person may render financial services for an unlicensed entity or act as a representative unless:
- They can provide certified confirmation of the representative agreement
- The FSP accepts responsibility for the representative's activities
- If previously debarred: complies with requirements for reappointment

The FSP must:
- Be satisfied at all times that its representatives (and their KIs) are competent
- Take reasonable steps to ensure representatives comply with codes of conduct and applicable laws
- **Maintain a register of representatives** (and KIs of juristic representatives), kept updated and available to the registrar

The register must contain:
- Each representative's name and business address
- Whether they act as employee or mandatory
- Categories in which they are competent to render financial services

**Section 14: Debarment**
If a representative no longer complies with fitness requirements, the FSP MUST:
- Withdraw authorisation to act
- Remove from the representative register
- Take steps to protect clients and conclude unconcluded business
- Inform the registrar **within 30 days** of the removal

### Part 4: Chapter IV — Codes of Conduct (Sections 15–16)

**Section 15:** The FSCA must publish Codes of Conduct for FSPs. Different codes may apply to different categories of FSPs.

**Section 16: Principles**
Codes must ensure FSPs:
- Act honestly, fairly, with due skill, care and diligence
- Have effective resources, procedures and technology
- Seek appropriate information from clients about their financial situation, experience and objectives
- Act with circumspection and treat clients fairly in conflicts of interest
- Comply with all applicable laws

Codes must contain provisions on:
- Adequate disclosure of material information (including conflicts of interest)
- Adequate record-keeping
- Avoidance of fraudulent/misleading advertising
- Proper safekeeping and protection of client funds
- Professional indemnity/fidelity insurance cover
- Other necessary matters

### Part 5: Chapter V — Duties of FSPs (Sections 17–19)

**Section 17: Compliance Officers**
Any FSP with more than one key individual OR one or more representatives MUST appoint a compliance officer.

Requirements:
- May be a director, member, auditor, trustee, principal officer, company secretary, or other suitable person
- Must be approved by the registrar
- The FSP must establish and maintain compliance procedures
- The compliance officer (or FSP if no CO) must submit compliance reports to the FSCA

**Section 18: Record Keeping**
FSPs must keep records for **minimum 5 years** on:
- Premature cancellations by clients
- Complaints received and resolution status
- Continued compliance with authorisation requirements
- Non-compliance cases and reasons
- Continued compliance by representatives

**Section 19: Accounting and Audit**
FSPs must:
- Maintain full accounting records monthly
- Prepare annual financial statements
- Have financial statements **audited by an approved external auditor**
- Submit audited financials to the FSCA **within 6 months of financial year end**
- Submit auditor's report confirming:
  - Amount of client money/assets held
  - That client money was kept separate from FSP's own money
  - Any other required information

The auditor must immediately report any material irregularity to the FSCA.

### 1. Dual Regulatory Framework for CASPs in South Africa

CASPs are regulated under TWO separate but concurrent frameworks:

| Framework | Regulator | Applicable Law | Focus |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| AML Regulation | Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) | FICA (Financial Intelligence Centre Act, 2001) | Anti-money laundering, customer due diligence |
| Financial Services | FSCA | FAIS Act, Conduct Standard 3A of 2023 | Advice, intermediary services, fit and proper |

### 2. FICA Definition of Crypto Asset
"A digital representation of perceived value that can be traded or transferred electronically within a community of users of the internet who consider it as a medium of exchange, unit of account or store of value and use it for payment or investment purposes, but does not include a digital representation of a fiat currency or a security."

### 3. FAIS Definition of Crypto Asset (Financial Product)
"A digital representation of value that:
- (a) is not issued by a central bank, but is capable of being traded, transferred or stored electronically by natural and legal persons for the purpose of payment, investment and other forms of utility;
- (b) applies cryptographic techniques; and
- (c) uses distributed ledger technology."

### 4. What Triggers FICA Registration?
As an accountable institution, a CASP is required to register with the FIC if it carries on the business of:
- (a) Exchanging crypto assets for fiat or vice versa
- (b) Exchanging one form of crypto asset for another
- (c) Conducting a transaction that transfers crypto assets from one address/account to another
- (d) Safekeeping/administration of crypto assets or instruments enabling control over them
- (e) Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer/sale of crypto assets

**BitWealth triggers FICA registration under (a) [ZAR → BTC], (c) [BTC transfers], and (d) [safekeeping/administration in VALR subaccounts].**

**Deadline for FICA registration:** Within **90 days** after the business has begun operating.

### 5. What Triggers FAIS Licensing?
Any entity providing **"advice"** or rendering **"intermediary services"** regarding crypto assets must be authorised as an FSP.

**BitWealth triggers FAIS licensing because:**
- It provides advice (recommends and implements the LTH PVR DCA strategy)
- It renders intermediary services (executes purchases of BTC on behalf of clients)
- It administers/safekeeps crypto assets (manages VALR subaccounts)

### 6. Regulated Activities Under FAIS (Crypto Relevant)

| Activity | Regulated Under FAIS? |
|----------|----------------------|
| Exchange (buy/sell crypto) | Yes — intermediary services |
| Custody (holding/administering crypto) | Yes — intermediary services |
| Borrowing/Lending | Yes — advice or intermediary services |
| Staking/Yield farming | Yes — advice or intermediary services |
| NFTs | Yes — treated as crypto assets |
| Direct token sales by issuers | Yes — treated as crypto assets |

### 7. Offshore Business / Reverse Solicitation
- Foreign entities actively soliciting SA business must register as external companies under the Companies Act
- Foreign entities must be licensed as FSPs by the FSCA (or seek an exemption)
- Reverse solicitation: unclear position — depends on extent of SA-sourced business

### 8. Licensing Timeline
- **FICA registration**: 90 days after commencing business
- **FAIS FSP licence**: Applications for CASPs opened **1 June 2023**
- **Processing time**: Up to **6 months** (can raise additional queries)
- **Backlog**: FSCA has advised that CASP applications are processed in a phased approach; current backlog exists

### 9. Application Fees (Effective from 1 October 2024)

| Category | Fee |
|----------|-----|
| Category I FSP | ZAR 2,697 |
| Category II (Discretionary FSP) | ZAR 16,313 |
| Category IIA (Hedge Fund FSP) | ZAR 16,313 |
| Category III (Administrative FSP) | ZAR 49,015 |
| Category IV (Assistance FSP) | ZAR 2,697 |

Discount: 10% if applying for multiple categories (20% discount for Category III).

### 10. Other Legal Requirements for CASPs in South Africa

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **FICA fit and proper requirements** | Standards of honesty and integrity, competence, operational ability, financial soundness, CPD |
| **Cash transaction reporting** | Report cash transactions exceeding ZAR 49,999.99 (section 28 FICA) |
| **Suspicious transaction reporting** | Must file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with FIC (section 29 FICA) |
| **Companies Act compliance** | SA companies: full compliance with Companies Act 2008. External companies: register within 20 business days and maintain at least one SA office |
| **Advertising** | All crypto advertisements must include: "Investing in crypto assets may result in the loss of capital" |
| **Travel Rule (Directive 9, in force 30 April 2025)** | Must collect and transmit originator and beneficiary information for all crypto transfers |

### 11. Travel Rule — Directive 9 (Effective 30 April 2025) ⚠️ NEW REQUIREMENT
The FIC issued Directive 9 implementing the FATF Travel Rule for crypto asset transfers:

| Transaction Value | Verification Requirement |
|------------------|------------------------|
| Under ZAR 5,000 | Basic info collected; full verification only if suspicious |
| ZAR 5,000 or more | Full mandatory verification (FICA due diligence) |
| Inbound from high-risk jurisdiction | Full verification regardless of amount |

**Applies to:** All domestic AND cross-border crypto transfers, regardless of amount.

**Information Required:** Originator (sender) and beneficiary (receiver) information must be securely transmitted between ordering CASP and receiving CASP.

## Critical Risks and Compliance Gaps for BitWealth

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| **Operating Unlicensed** | BitWealth may already be rendering financial services in respect of crypto assets. Section 7 of FAIS prohibits unlicensed FSP activities. Transactions by unlicensed providers may be voided. | Apply for the FAIS licence as soon as possible. In the interim, consider whether a conditional exemption is available. |
| **Client Asset Segregation** | BitWealth must hold client money in a separate, ring-fenced account. This is verified annually by the auditor. | Open a dedicated client trust/money account before applying. |
| **FICA Non-Registration** | CASPs must register with FIC within 90 days of commencing business. | Register with the FIC immediately if not already done. |
| **Key Individual RE Exam** | Key individuals must pass RE 1 exam before managing/overseeing financial services. This exam requires preparation. | Register for RE 1 immediately at the Insurance Institute of SA or other approved body. |
| **No Compliance Officer** | Cannot operate as an FSP without an approved compliance officer (once licensed + have reps or multiple KIs). | Engage an external compliance practice. |
| **Travel Rule (Directive 9)** | All crypto transfers require originator and beneficiary information to be collected and transmitted from 30 April 2025. VALR (as the counterparty exchange) will also be subject to this. | Implement Travel Rule data collection in the onboarding/KYC process and in the transaction monitoring system. |
| **Omnibus Account Model** | BitWealth uses VALR omnibus subaccounts. The FSCA may scrutinise whether this is proper client asset segregation. | Document the subaccount structure clearly in the application (FSP 1 Section 7 and FSP 7 Section 4). |
| **Advertising Warning** | All crypto advertisements must include the capital loss warning. | Ensure all marketing materials, website, and app include the mandatory disclaimer. |
| **Professional Indemnity Insurance** | FSP 7 requires PI/Fidelity insurance. For a CASP handling client crypto, this is a significant and potentially costly requirement. | Obtain PI and fidelity cover; note that CASP-specific cover may be available from specialist insurers. |
