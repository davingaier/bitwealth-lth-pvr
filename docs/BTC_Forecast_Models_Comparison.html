BTC Diminishing-Return Forecast Models

A side-by-side view of four ways to temper future Bitcoin return expectations while preserving the historical back-test story in the ABC One Pager.

Prepared for model selection

Forecast methodology draft
NOTE
These forecasts are illustrative planning models only. They are designed to reduce unrealistic expectations created by historical BTC back-tests. They are not expected returns, financial advice, or a guarantee of future Bitcoin performance.
Historical Cycle Inputs

The core observation is simple: Bitcoin bottom-to-top cycle returns have compressed materially over time. Any forward-looking client material should therefore avoid carrying past cycle outcomes into the future without an explicit diminishing-return adjustment.

Four Model Options
Option 1

Log-Linear / Exponential Decay

MathematicalSimple fit

Fits a straight line through the logarithm of recent cycle returns, then projects the declining curve forward.

ln(return) = a + b * cycle
Uses cycles 2-4 as the fitted anchor so the extreme first cycle remains context instead of dominating the forecast.
Pros
  • Easy to explain and audit.
  • Directly models diminishing returns.
  • Good first model for internal review.
Cons
  • Sensitive to the extreme first cycle.
  • Only four data points.
  • Can become too conservative.
Option 2

Power-Law Maturation

BTC-nativeMaturation curve

Treats Bitcoin as a maturing network asset where early gains compress quickly, then the decline becomes more gradual.

ln(return) = a - b * ln(cycle)
Best when the narrative is market maturation rather than a fixed decay rate per cycle.
Pros
  • Fits adoption and network-growth language.
  • Less likely to trend to zero too quickly.
  • Useful for long-horizon discussion.
Cons
  • More abstract for clients.
  • Assumes cycles remain comparable.
  • Can look more precise than justified.
Option 3

Scenario Haircut Model

GovernanceScenario range

Applies explicit conservative, base, and optimistic haircuts to the prior cycle return instead of claiming a precise fit.

next return = prior return * haircut
Uses 20%, 35%, and 50% illustrative haircuts for conservative, base, and optimistic cases.
Pros
  • Very easy for clients to understand.
  • Avoids false precision.
  • Simple to govern and update.
Cons
  • Assumption-driven.
  • Needs formal approval of haircut levels.
  • Less mathematically elegant.
Option 4

Hybrid: Model Anchor + Scenarios

BalancedRecommended draft

Uses the recent-cycle Option 1 fit as the mathematical anchor, then presents conservative, base, and optimistic bands around it.

scenario = log-linear base * band factor
Uses 65%, 100%, and 135% of the fitted base projection, capped below the previous cycle.
Pros
  • Defensible mathematical core.
  • Client-friendly scenario framing.
  • Best fit for ABC One Pager disclosure.
Cons
  • Still depends on model assumptions.
  • Requires clear methodology notes.
  • More complex than a single model.

Decision Guidance

Choose Option 1 if...

You want the simplest defensible mathematical model and are comfortable explaining that the forecast is a fitted decay curve.

Choose Option 2 if...

You want the language to centre on Bitcoin's market maturation and long-term adoption rather than a fixed cycle decay rate.

Choose Option 3 if...

You want maximum client clarity and governance control, even if the assumptions are more explicitly subjective.

Choose Option 4 if...

You want a balanced approach: a mathematical anchor for discipline, wrapped in conservative/base/optimistic scenarios for communication.
